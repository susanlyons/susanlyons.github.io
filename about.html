Our Mission

The mission of Women in Measurement, Inc. is to support the advancement of women in educational measurement. We work to amplify the voices of women--and minoritized females in particular--and to provide structures of support for women throughout their careers in academia, industry, non-profits, and the public sector. Women in Measurement, Inc. seeks to understand and dismantle systems of oppression that have led to disproportionate access and representation at the highest levels of our field.

Leadership Team

Susan Lyons
Executive Director

Susan is an independent consultant who works with states, districts, testing companies, and educational non-profits to improve practice in educational assessment and school accountability. Susan has particular interest in leveraging the power of assessment to interrupt systemic inequities. Susan's work ranges from the design and use of classroom assessments to re-imagining large-scale testing and accountability. In addition to her consulting work, Susan holds a part-time faculty appointment at Boston College.

Jenn Dunn
Chief Operating Officer

Jenn is the Chief Assessment and Data Officer at Questar Assessments Inc. where she is responsible for guiding the strategic direction of the assessment and data solutions for the company. Foundational to Jenn's beliefs is the notion that assessment content and data can be used to communicate a compelling, informative and actionable story about students. Drawing on over fifteen years of experience, Jenn is passionate about finding ways in which assessment principles and information can be used to inform educational policies and instructional strategies to disrupt gender and racial disparities.

Kadriye Ercikan

Kadriye Ercikan is Vice President of Research and Measurement Sciences at the Educational Testing Service and Professor Emerita at the University of British Columbia. She is responsible ETS’s foundational and applied research and data analysis and psychometric support of ETS’s major testing products and contracts. Her research focuses on designing and validating assessments of complex thinking, the assessment of linguistic minorities, and fairness and validity issues in cross-cultural and international assessments.

Ercikan is a Fellow of the International Academy of Education. Her research has resulted in six books, four special issues of refereed journals and over 100 publications. She was awarded the AERA Division D Significant Contributions to Educational Measurement and Research Methodology recognition for another
co-edited volume, Generalizing from Educational Research: Beyond Qualitative and Quantitative
Polarization, and received an Early Career Award from the University of British Columbia. Ercikan has been selected to serve as the NCME Book Series Editor (2021-2026). 

Ellen Forte

Ellen Forte (Ph.D., University of Iowa) is the CEO & Chief Scientist of edCount, LLC, the consulting firm she founded in 2003. Dr. Forte’s work focuses on validity evaluation and on policies for how students, including those with disabilities and English learners, engage in instructional and assessment contexts. She particularly specializes in designing, developing, and evaluating assessments for alignment quality.
Dr. Forte serves on the NCME Board of Directors; the TACs for six US states, ACT, Project Lead the Way, and the UK Standards and Testing Agency; and on the editorial boards for Educational Measurement (5th edition), Educational Measurement: Issues and Practice, and Applied Measurement in Education. Dr. Forte wrote The Administrators’ Guide to Federal Programs for English Learners, first published in 2010 and revised in 2017, which addresses intersections among civil rights laws, education policy, and measurement and evaluation issues for English learners.

Prior to founding edCount, Dr. Forte was the Title I evaluator in the Connecticut Department of Education, the Director of Student Assessment for the Baltimore City Public Schools, and a project director with vendors in the teacher certification and student assessment sectors. 

Jennifer Randall

Jennifer Randall is an associate professor in the Research, Educational Measurement, and Psychometrics program, Director of Evaluation in the Center for Educational Assessment, and Associate Dean of Academic Affairs in the College of Education at the University of Massachusetts. She began her career as a public school teacher in secondary history working with historically marginalized students. She has (and continues to) advocate for socially just assessment practices through her service on local, state, and federal committees. Currently, she is particularly interested in the differential negative impact of both large and small-scale assessments on historically marginalized populations in the U.S. and abroad; and the ways in which a culturally responsive, anti-racist approach to instruction and assessment can mitigate these negative outcomes. She teaches courses in measurement theory, scale development, statistics, and classroom assessment.

Ye Tong

Dr. Ye Tong is the Vice President of Psychometric and Research Services at Pearson. In this role, she manages all the psychometric and measurement services teams within the Pearson Assessments organization, including the Pearson VUE (professional and IT certification, US regulatory licensure, and admissions markets) and School Assessment (US K–12 school market) groups.

Dr. Tong is a nationally recognized measurement expert with many presentations and publications in areas of equating, vertical scaling, computer adaptive testing, standard setting, and educational policy. Dr. Tong is currently the President for National Council on Measurement in Education (NCME). She also serves on the Editorial Boards for Journal of Educational Measurement, and Educational Measurement: Issues and Practice.
